Flagel Lab News

  • The Flagel Lab is excited to welcome Princess Felix, a student in the Neuroscience Graduate Program, who will be conducting her dissertation research in the Flagel Lab. Princess will be focusing on the role of stress in cue-motivated behaviors and, specifically, the role of the glucocorticoid receptor in mediating "stress-reward" interactions.

  • Dr. Flagel was interviewed by Gil Eapen for the Scientific Sense podcast for her research on the neural circuitry that contributes to individual differences in vulnerability to mental illness. The episode can be found here: https://open.spotify.com/episode/1okgUsZdV5twUl2jaH4KOc.

  • The Flagel Lab is excited to have been awarded two new grants from the National Institute on Drug Abuse (NIDA): an R21 (DA052594) to investigate the role of the glucocorticoid receptor in cue-motivated behaviors; and an R01 (DA054094) to investigate the role of the hypothalamic-thalamic-striatal circuit in cue-motivated behaviors.

  • Dr. Flagel is the recipient of the 2020 Society of Biological Psychiatry A.E. Bennett Basic Research Award. Here is a video of the virtual award ceremony: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oVrRLjxlPI0.

  • Dr. Flagel was interviewed for a recent article in Scientific American that features our work. The article is titled, “Scientists Spot Addiction-Associated Circuit in Rats”, and has been published online.

  • Dr. Flagel was happy to work with Health in Our Hands (@heatlh_io_hands) and students from the University of Michigan School of Public Health (Erika Holiday, Ross Baiers and Madison Hafitz) on this video which will be embedded into a new addiction unit to expose young students to the work and careers of scientists.

  • Amanda Iglesias was awarded a National Science Foundation (NSF) Graduate Research Fellowship to study subcortical hypothalamic circuitry involved in cue-reward learning. The NSF Fellowship will support her from 2019-2022!
